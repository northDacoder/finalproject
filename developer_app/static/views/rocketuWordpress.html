RocketU is part of RocketSpace, a leading technology campus located in the heart of San Francisco 's booming tech community. That places students at the epicenter of some of the hottest startups and companies on the planet! RocketU was established to produce top-performing developers and entrepreneurs. We're here to train the next round of world-changers through the application of coding languages and know-how, in today's tech-centric world. RocketU is not just another developer bootcamp. In addition to learning the latest programming languages and building a full stack product to showcase to employers, you will be: - Surrounded by amazing startups and companies looking for talent just like you. - Joining one of the most innovative technology campuses in the world. - Immersed in events with world class speakers presenting the latest technologies. RocketSpace's alumni include Uber, Spotify, Leap Motion, Snip.it and Podio; with corporate innovation partners including Samsung, British Airways, Microsoft, Pearson and General Motors.

Experienced faculty & mentors

Rudy Mutter

Rudy has been obsessed with technology since he got his first PC in ’91 as a kid and has been developing websites & games since. As a co-founder and the technical director of Yeti he currently spends his days in the Yeti Cave architecting web and mobile application solutions for clients, as well as teaching the latest RocketU class how to be Djangonauts.

Tony Scherba

Tony is a passionate builder of things. After creating websites and apps for a decade and graduating with a degree in Computer Science and Business, Tony went on to found Yeti, San Francisco’s premiere development studio. Yeti serves as RocketU’s teaching staff and curriculum advisors, providing real world examples and expertise to the students. Tony is a frequent lecturer and actively makes curriculum contributions.

Baylee Feore

Baylee is a former management consultant who decided to throw it all out the window and attend a web development bootcamp. She has been building projects in Django ever since and loving it. When not working on web and mobile products at Yeti or teaching students at RocketU, she runs Out for Undergrad, a non-profit that educates LGBT undergrads in the transition to careers in business and tech.

Randall Koutnik

Randall is a “Full stack” geek, with experience in IT, devops, over a dozen programming languages and several tabletop RPGs. He’s loved building things from a young age, starting with Legos and expanding into programming. In addition to teaching at RocketU, he’s a full-time developer at Zaption and part-time consultant.

Nick Roper

Known by students as one of the most patient, approachable teachers in tech. As a veteran software engineer and seasoned senior trainer, Nick has been providing development consulting, mentoring & developer training globally for over 20 years. When he isn’t teaching he is learning, building apps, tipping a pint, listening to music and enjoying his family.
